# Behavioral Question Bank

Use this file when mapping a story to question families, selecting the best angle, or tailoring behavior prep for a specific company.

## How to Use This Bank

For each story:
1. Pick the 2-4 best-fit categories below.
2. Choose the question wording closest to the interviewer's ask.
3. Adjust the framing, not the facts.
4. Be ready for follow-ups on trade-offs, metrics, and what you would do differently.

## Core Question Families

### Leadership / Initiative

Common questions:
- Tell me about a time you took initiative without being asked.
- Tell me about a time you led something outside your formal scope.
- Tell me about a time you raised the bar on a process or system.

Strong stories show:
- ownership before authority
- clear problem definition
- buy-in from others
- measurable improvement

Common follow-ups:
- Why did you decide to step in?
- How did you get support?
- What resistance did you face?

### Conflict / Disagreement

Common questions:
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate.
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager or partner team.
- Tell me about a time you had to handle conflict on a project.

Strong stories show:
- professional disagreement, not personality drama
- listening and clarification
- principled reasoning
- preserved or improved working relationship

Common follow-ups:
- What did the other person care about?
- What did you change after hearing their view?
- What happened to the relationship afterward?

### Failure / Mistake / Setback

Common questions:
- Tell me about a time you failed.
- Tell me about a time you made a mistake.
- Tell me about a project that did not go as planned.

Strong stories show:
- personal ownership
- fast detection and recovery
- concrete learning
- changed process or behavior afterward

Common follow-ups:
- How did you realize it was failing?
- What did you do first?
- What would you do differently now?

### Ambiguity / Incomplete Information

Common questions:
- Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information.
- Tell me about a time requirements were unclear.
- Tell me about a time you operated in ambiguity.

Strong stories show:
- how you reduced uncertainty
- decision criteria
- risk management
- a reversible or staged approach when needed

Common follow-ups:
- What data did you wish you had?
- Why was your decision reasonable at the time?
- How did you de-risk the choice?

### Influence Without Authority

Common questions:
- Tell me about a time you influenced people without direct authority.
- Tell me about a time you had to convince another team.
- Tell me about a time you drove alignment across stakeholders.

Strong stories show:
- understanding of different incentives
- clear communication
- durable alignment, not just winning an argument
- momentum after the decision

Common follow-ups:
- Who was hardest to convince?
- What evidence changed minds?
- How did you keep momentum after agreement?

### Cross-functional Collaboration

Common questions:
- Tell me about a time you worked cross-functionally.
- Tell me about a time you partnered with product, design, SRE, or another team.
- Tell me about a time collaboration was essential to success.

Strong stories show:
- dependency management
- role clarity
- communication rhythm
- shared outcome across functions

Common follow-ups:
- What was your role versus theirs?
- How did you keep everyone aligned?
- Where did misunderstandings happen?

### Customer / Stakeholder Focus

Common questions:
- Tell me about a time you advocated for the customer.
- Tell me about a time you balanced user needs against technical constraints.
- Tell me about a time you improved an experience for an internal or external customer.

Strong stories show:
- understanding of who the customer was
- concrete pain point
- trade-off between ideal and feasible
- meaningful improvement in experience

Common follow-ups:
- How did you know this mattered?
- What trade-offs did you make?
- How did you measure success?

### Prioritization / Tight Deadline

Common questions:
- Tell me about a time you had to prioritize under pressure.
- Tell me about a time you met a tough deadline.
- Tell me about a time everything felt urgent.

Strong stories show:
- explicit prioritization logic
- scope control
- communication under pressure
- outcome without heroics as the only strategy

Common follow-ups:
- What did you deprioritize?
- How did you communicate trade-offs?
- What would you have done with more time?

### Learning / Adaptability

Common questions:
- Tell me about a time you had to learn something quickly.
- Tell me about a time you entered a new domain.
- Tell me about a time you adapted after feedback or change.

Strong stories show:
- deliberate learning plan
- fast application
- humility and iteration
- compounding improvement

Common follow-ups:
- How did you ramp up?
- What did you misunderstand at first?
- How did you know you were improving?

### Technical Judgment / Trade-offs

Common questions:
- Tell me about a difficult technical decision.
- Tell me about a time you had to balance speed and quality.
- Tell me about a time you chose between two valid approaches.

Strong stories show:
- decision criteria
- alternatives considered
- cost, risk, or scalability trade-offs
- why the chosen path fit the context

Common follow-ups:
- What other options did you consider?
- Why not the simpler or faster approach?
- What signals would have made you choose differently?

### Feedback / Mentorship

Common questions:
- Tell me about a time you received hard feedback.
- Tell me about a time you coached someone.
- Tell me about a time you helped someone improve.

Strong stories show:
- openness to feedback
- specific change in behavior
- empathy
- durable improvement

Common follow-ups:
- What was difficult about the feedback?
- What did you change first?
- How do you know it worked?

### Resilience / Incident Response

Common questions:
- Tell me about a time you handled a production issue.
- Tell me about a stressful situation and how you responded.
- Tell me about a time you recovered from a setback quickly.

Strong stories show:
- calm prioritization
- communication during uncertainty
- technical action plus stakeholder updates
- prevention afterward

Common follow-ups:
- What was the first thing you did?
- How did you communicate status?
- What prevention work came out of it?

## Non-STAR but Common

These questions still benefit from story-bank prep, but they are not full STAR answers:

### Tell Me About Yourself
- Use `present -> past -> future`
- keep a project-summary intro, a reusable high-level answer core, and optional short/long technical deep dives
- connect current strengths to the target role

### Why This Company / Why This Role
- connect background to team problems
- mention one or two real company-specific reasons
- avoid generic compliments

### Biggest Strength
- pick one strength supported by 1-2 stories
- keep it specific and evidenced

### Biggest Weakness
- choose a real but manageable weakness
- show what you changed
- include evidence of improvement

## Company Overlays

### Amazon

Most common themes:
- Customer Obsession
- Ownership
- Invent and Simplify
- Are Right, A Lot
- Dive Deep
- Bias for Action
- Deliver Results

Typical question angles:
- Tell me about a time you had to earn trust.
- Tell me about a time you disagreed and committed.
- Tell me about a time you went deep into data or root cause.
- Tell me about a time you delivered under constraints.

Amazon framing tips:
- make ownership explicit
- use metrics whenever possible
- be crisp about customer impact
- emphasize the exact principle without naming it awkwardly every sentence

### Google

Most common themes:
- collaboration
- ambiguity
- learning
- leadership without title
- sound judgment

Typical question angles:
- Tell me about a time you had to make progress without clear direction.
- Tell me about a time you worked with people who saw the problem differently.
- Tell me about a time you learned something fast to solve a problem.

Google framing tips:
- show reasoning, not just execution
- highlight collaboration and curiosity
- mention trade-offs and what you learned
- avoid sounding overly scripted

### Meta

Most common themes:
- impact
- ownership
- speed
- iteration
- prioritization under pressure

Typical question angles:
- Tell me about a time you moved fast.
- Tell me about a time you delivered high impact with limited time.
- Tell me about a time you made a pragmatic trade-off.

Meta framing tips:
- lead with scale or impact
- show decisive action
- explain what you shipped and what you learned
- avoid bloated setup

## Story Selection Heuristics

Prefer stories that have:
- a clear before/after
- visible trade-offs or tension
- at least one proof point
- a specific personal contribution
- a follow-up-ready detail on metrics, alternatives, or alignment

Avoid stories that are:
- mostly team effort with unclear personal ownership
- all setup and no action
- impossible to verify under probing follow-ups
- emotionally messy without a professional takeaway
